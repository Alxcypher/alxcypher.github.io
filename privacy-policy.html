FitShift Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 27, 2026

What FitShift Does

FitShift is a Chrome extension that helps runners find the right shoe size across different brands. It compares size charts and community fit data so you can shop with confidence.

Data We Collect

  • Shoe preferences — brands, models, sizes, and gender selections you save in the extension.
  • Foot measurements — foot length and width, if you choose to enter them.
  • Fit reports — size comparison ratings you submit (e.g., "runs small").
  • Google account info — your name, email address, and profile picture, only if you sign in to enable cloud sync.

How Data Is Stored

  • Locally — all data is stored on your device in IndexedDB via an embedded SQLite database. The extension works fully offline.
  • Cloud (optional) — if you sign in with Google, your shoe data and fit reports are synced to a Supabase-hosted PostgreSQL database so you can access them across devices. Data is protected by row-level security policies that restrict access to your account only.

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • Browsing history or page content beyond shoe product detection on supported retailer sites.
  • Payment information, physical addresses, or any financial data.
  • Analytics, tracking pixels, or advertising identifiers.

Third-Party Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third parties. Your data is used solely to provide the FitShift size comparison service.

Third-Party Services

  • Google OAuth — used for sign-in authentication. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Supabase — used for cloud data storage and authentication. Subject to Supabase's Privacy Policy.

How to Delete Your Data

  • Local data — right-click the FitShift icon in Chrome, select "Remove from Chrome," and confirm. This deletes all local data.
  • Cloud data — sign out from the extension's Settings tab. You can also email the developer (below) to request full deletion of cloud-stored data.

Permissions Explained

  • <all_urls> — allows FitShift to detect running shoe product pages on any retailer website, not just the major brands with dedicated content scripts.
  • storage — used to persist your local database and sync preferences.
  • identity — required for Google sign-in via Chrome's identity API.
  • sidePanel — the extension UI opens in Chrome's side panel.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy or want to request data deletion, please open an issue on the FitShift GitHub repository or email the developer directly.
